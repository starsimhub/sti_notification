# ZIMPHIA 2015-16 Table 18.4.A — Syphilis prevalence

Prevalence of syphilis (ever infected = treponemal+ ; active = treponemal+ AND
nontreponemal+) among persons aged 15-64, by sex and selected demographic
characteristics. Source: PHIA 2015-16 final report.

**National totals (15-64).** Trep+ 2.7% / active 0.8%. Trep:active ratio ≈ 3.4.
F slightly higher than M on both (3.0 vs 2.4 trep; 1.0 vs 0.6 active).

## What this implies for the mixture story

- **The geographic urban/rural mixture story is not supported.** Urban-rural
  spread is zero; provincial spread is ≤ 2-3×. The "hot district × cold district"
  hypothesis would need 5-10× spread to explain a 1% national mean as a blend.
- **The real hot subgroups are behavioural and demographic, not geographic.**
  Divorced/separated/widowed women: ~5-7% trep, 2-3% active. HIV+: 7.1% trep,
  2.9% active. Low-education: ~10% trep.
- **The 15-49 bracket lands at 2.0/0.8.** The model's loose target band (5-10%
  trep, 1-3% active) sits *above* the survey total — the band was calibrated to
  the 15-64 column but the under-49 cut is tighter. This re-frames how hot the
  model can be without overshooting.

## Implications for our calibration framing

- The model's FSW node is the behavioural hot subgroup. The Matabeleland +
  divorced/widowed + low-education + HIV+ cells all run 5-7% trep, 1.5-3%
  active — these are the empirical "hot subgroups" we should compare the FSW
  node against, not the national average.
- A weighted "behavioural-mixture" story is more defensible than a geographic
  one: ~3-5% of women in the hot behavioural subgroup, ~95% in the lower-risk
  bulk. This is structurally similar to the FSW / non-FSW split the model
  already has — so the question becomes whether the model's prop_f0 + transmission
  dynamics already implicitly produce this composition correctly.
